New York City stands as a towering metropolis, a vibrant mosaic of iconic landmarks that represent the city's history, architectural grandeur, and cultural energy. From the majestic Empire State Building to the refined Museum of Modern Art (MOMA) and the sprawling Central Park, these structures are more than just sights - they are symbols that embody the very spirit of New York City.


Preservation

New York City, a dynamic metropolis constantly evolving, is also fiercely committed to protecting its past through a robust system of landmark preservation, ensuring iconic buildings and neighborhoods remain a vital part of its identity. This blog explores the key mechanisms that safeguard the city's architectural heritage, from the landmark designation process to community activism and the vital role of the Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC).

The Smell of Plants

  1. Plants produce a variety of scents for different reasons. Some use their fragrance to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, while others emit strong odors to repel pests or warn other plants of danger.
  2. Lavender is known for its calming scent, while the corpse flower is infamous for its foul smell, which attracts pollinators that feed on decaying matter.
  3. Some plants, like mint and basil, release their aroma when their leaves are touched or crushed.
  4. The smell of freshly cut grass is actually a plant distress signal.
  5. Many flowers use scent to attract specific pollinators, such as bees, moths, or bats.
  6. Some plants, like skunk cabbage, can even generate heat to help spread their scent further.
